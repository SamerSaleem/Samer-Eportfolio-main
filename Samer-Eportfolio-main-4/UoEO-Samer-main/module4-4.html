Unit 4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques



In this unit, We've been introduced us to the new module we are starting and explained the following topics:

To create a threat model for a large international bank based in the UK, we will employ the STRIDE framework as outlined in Shostack (2018), integrating insights from the Threat Modeling Manifesto, the OWASP Threat Modeling Cookbook, and the ATT&CK libraries. This model will help identify and categorize potential threats to the bank's systems and infrastructure, ensuring a comprehensive approach to security.

Scope and Key Assets
The scope of this threat model covers the entire operational landscape of the bank, including online banking platforms, customer data repositories, internal communication networks, and financial transaction systems. Key assets include customer financial data, employee credentials, banking transaction records, proprietary algorithms, and IT infrastructure.

STRIDE Analysis
Spoofing involves attackers impersonating legitimate users or systems to gain unauthorized access. Potential threats include stolen credentials, phishing attacks, and fraudulent transactions. Tampering refers to unauthorized modifications to data, such as altering transaction records or customer data, leading to fraud and loss of trust. Repudiation threats occur when users deny actions such as unauthorized transactions, highlighting the need for robust logging. Information Disclosure includes leakage of sensitive data, potentially caused by vulnerabilities like inadequate encryption. Denial of Service (DoS) attacks target the bank's online platforms, potentially causing customer dissatisfaction. Elevation of Privilege occurs when attackers gain unauthorized access, potentially leading to severe financial fraud.

Mitigation Strategies and ATT&CK Integration
The bank should employ strong authentication, encryption, and logging mechanisms. Regular security assessments, including penetration testing, will help address tampering and privilege escalation. Aligning with the ATT&CK framework ensures the bank understands attacker tactics and stays ahead of threats.

In conclusion, applying the STRIDE framework and leveraging additional resources helps ensure a comprehensive and proactive security posture for the bank.
