Module Introduction - Seminar


We talked about CIA of Data, the necessity of following and building policy on the foundation of the triad of CIA. Learning about the regulations and laws for data protection
under the GDPR, in addition, the job roles in the field of Cyber security.
The reading list was: Anderson, R. (2008) Security Engineering: A Guide to Building
Dependable Distributed Systems. 3rd ed. Indiana: Wiley & Sons. Chapter 2. Chapters 21 and 28 are useful for the assignment. Accessed here.

The readings provide a comprehensive perspective on risk management and the impact of digitalization. Olson and Desheng (2020), in Enterprise Risk Management Models, emphasize the importance of developing robust models to manage and predict risks across enterprises, detailing how risk is quantified and the strategic responses necessary to mitigate it.

Stoneburner et al. (2002) present the NIST SP 800-30 guide, which offers a foundational framework for risk management in information technology systems. This guide outlines the risk assessment process, including identifying vulnerabilities, assessing potential threats, and implementing risk mitigation strategies, which remain highly relevant in today's cybersecurity landscape.

Sørensen (2018) explores the dual nature of digitalization, acknowledging that while it presents opportunities for innovation and competitiveness, it also introduces risks, particularly in regulatory and competitive contexts. The article underscores the need for businesses to adapt to digital transformations while managing associated risks.

Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019) discuss the risks specific to the digitalization of business models. They highlight the challenges businesses face, such as cyber threats, data privacy concerns, and the shifting landscape of digital economies. The paper stresses that while digitalization can drive efficiency and growth, it requires proactive risk management strategies to ensure sustainable success.

These readings collectively emphasize that digital transformation brings both opportunities and risks, requiring structured risk management approaches across technological and business domains.

References:

Olson, D.L. & Desheng, D.W. (2020). Enterprise Risk Management Models. Berlin, Germany: Springer.

Stoneburner, G., Goguen, A. & Feringa, A. (2002). SP 800-30: Risk Management Guide for Information Technology Systems. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

Sørensen, B.T. (2018). Digitalisation: An Opportunity or a Risk? Journal of European Competition Law & Practice, 9(6), pp. 349–350.

Kovaitė, K. & Stankevičienė, J. (2019). Risks of digitalisation of business models. In Proceedings of the 6th International Scientific Conference Contemporary Issues in Business, Management and Economics Engineering ‘2019.
